High School Dropout Rate

Background

Campus Dashboard Metrics

As the ultimate goal of high school campuses is to graduate students ready for college within four years, many states are including a graduation rate metric into their dashboards. In addition, during focus groups conducted with educators, the High School Dropout Rate metric, as presented in screen shots of the campus dashboard, was considered useful by 97% of those surveyed.

Primary Metric

  • High School Dropout Rate: percentage of students who have left the school and are not enrolled in another school.

Related Metrics

  • High School Graduation Rate: percentage of students graduating within four years of entering the ninth grade.

Status Definition

This metric displays the percentage of students who have the left the school and are not enrolled in another school.

The Status indicator is determined by a campus-specific threshold (campus goal) which is set at the district level. The default dropout rate is 10%.

Delta Definition

The Delta column is the absolute value of the difference between the campus metric percentage (Status column) and the campus goal percentage (Campus Goal column).  If the campus percentage is greater than or equal to the campus goal, the delta percentage is displayed in red.  If the campus percentage is less than the campus goal, the delta percentage is shown in black.

Periodicity

At a minimum, upload the data for this metric at least annually, prior to the beginning of the school year. Given that students can transfer any time throughout the year, best practice is to upload the data monthly to capture all transfers.

Business Rules

The High School Dropout Rate metric displays the percentage of students dropping out of school who were enrolled in a given school year in a public school in grades 9-12 and did not do any of the following:

  • Return to public school the following fall
  • Get expelled
  • Graduate
  • Receive GED
  • Continue school outside the public school system
  • Begin college
  • Decease

Data Assumptions

  • Metric is computed for prior year.
  • All students are assigned a graduation plan for grades 9-12.
  • All students are assigned a cohort.
  • Cohort consists of students who first attended ninth grade in 2004-2005.
  • Any student who transferred into the 2004-05 cohort is added to it, and any student who transfers out of the 2004-05 cohort is subtracted from it.

Computed Values

The cohort is the currently enrolled students (grades 9th - 12th) at the time of the last upload of data. The cohort excludes:

  • Students who previously enrolled, but left or transferred to another campus.
  • Students who do not match student data due to SSID.
  • Students who do not have complete transcripts.

Calculation Notes

Cohort = 9th grade students in the expected year of graduation who:

  • Graduated (Leaver code 01) anytime over the last four years prior to  Aug 31 of the current year
  • Dropout (Leaver code 98) at any time over the last four years prior to Aug 31 of the current year (does not account for possible GED)
  • Summer dropout (no show): of the prior 12th graders, completed prior year and not identified as a leaver, but is not enrolled in the next Fall school year (by the last Friday in September – Sept 30th could be practically used)
  • Continuers (still enrolled in high school) – of the prior 12th graders, completed prior year and not identified as a leaver, but is enrolled in the next Fall school year (by the last Friday in September – Sept 30th could be practically used)

Note: Leavers other than graduates (01) or dropout (98) are excluded from the cohort

Longitudinal  Dropout Rate*

Dropout Rate = Dropped out (leaver code 98 + Summer dropout)/ (Graduated + Continuers + Dropouts + Summer Dropouts)

Implementation Considerations

Student Identity

Maintaining a correct and consistent student identity is at the center of any education data system. Most systems use some sort of unique identifier. However, sometimes this identifier is entered incorrectly or sometimes different systems use different identifiers.

The UDM XML supports the interchange of multiple types of identifiers. The StudentReference is a complex type within the UDM to maintain the referential integrity of the student (that is, ensuring that the data associated with each student is accurately associated with the right student). The complex type of the student reference assists with implementing the accurate matching algorithm to identify a student by utilizing any of the individual attributes (e.g., Student Unique State ID, Student ID, Campus Local ID (with Campus ID), Name and Birth Date). For example, if the Student Unique State ID is unknown, you can find the student’s identity by their Student ID, First Name, Last Name and Birth Date.

Annual Dropout Rate

Three annual dropout rate indicators are shown:

Annual Dropout Rate (Grades 7-8)

  • This includes only grades 7 and 8. This rate is used in determining a campus accountability rating under standard procedures (for campuses that have one or both of those grades) or the district's rating. It is calculated as follows:
  • Number of dropouts in grades 7 and 8 during the 2007-08 school year / Number of grade 7 and 8 students who were in attendance at any time during the 2007-08 school year

Annual Dropout Rate (Grades 7-12)

  • This includes grades 7 through 12. This rate is used in determining a campus or charter operator accountability rating under AEA procedures (for campuses or charters that have one or more of those grades).  It is calculated as follows:
  • Number of dropouts in grades 7 through 12 during the 2007-08 school year  /  Number of grade 7-12 students who were in attendance at any time during the 2007-08 school year

Annual Dropout Rate (Grades 9-12)

  • This includes grades 9 through 12. This measure shows the dropout rates for the high school grades. It is a report-only measure and is not used in determining accountability ratings. It is calculated as follows:
  • Number of dropouts in grades 9 through 12 during the 2007-08 school year / Number of grade 9-12 students who were in attendance at any time during the 2007-08 school year

Note: For the accountability ratings of 2007 and 2008, the School Leaver Provision was in place to aid in the transition to a more rigorous dropout definition. This provision exempted schools and districts from having their accountability rating lowered due to their annual dropout rate. This provision is no longer in effect for the 2009 ratings.

NCES Annual (Event) Dropout Rate

  • Number of students who dropped out (Leaver code 98) during the school year across all grades/Number of students who were enrolled on the Fall snapshot day ( Last Friday in October)
  • Number of students who dropped out (leaver code 98) during the school year across all grades/Number of students who were enrolled anytime during the year

HB3 Exclusions (Effective 2011-2012 School Year)

The following five groups of students are excluded from dropout counts when evaluating dropout and completion data for accreditation and performance ratings:

  • Previous dropouts
  • Dropouts from whom school districts are not receiving state Foundation School Program (FSP) funds (usually because the students are being served fewer than two hours of instruction per day)
  • Students who have been ordered by courts to attend GED programs but have not earned GED certificates
  • Students incarcerated in facilities not served by public schools
  • Refugees and asylees

Aggregate Metric Inclusions and Exclusions of Special Education Students

Each campus's aggregate metrics may include or exclude the special education population, depending on the intended purpose for a specific metric and campus goal.

The Historical Graph allows the user to see historical data across multiple timeframes.  As time passes, there are many bars to be displayed; therefore, the slider on the bottom allows the user to adjust the number of bars displayed as well as the time period shown.  The timeframes for this metric were chosen based on educator feedback.
