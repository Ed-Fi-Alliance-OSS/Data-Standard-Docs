Teacher Certification by Subject Area

Background

Campus Dashboard Metrics

All 50 states require classroom educators to hold a state certification in order to teach. The Teacher Certification metric helps to ensure that all teachers are qualified. Additionally, in focus with educators, the Teacher Certification by Subject Area metric was seen as useful by 89% of respondents. 

Primary Metric

  • Teacher Certification by Subject Area: percentage of teachers certified to teach the subject and/or grade level they are teaching in each of the four core subject areas, as well as bilingual, ESL, and special education

Related Metric

  • Teacher Education: percentage of teachers holding masters or doctorate degrees.

Delta Definition

The Delta column is the absolute value of the difference between the campus certification percentage (Status column) and the campus goal (Campus Goal column). If the certification percentage is below the campus goal, the delta percentage is displayed in red. If the certification percentage is equal to or above the campus goal, the delta percentage is displayed in black.

Business Rules

Business Rules

The Teacher Certification by Subject Area metric displays the percentage of teachers who are certified in the subject area they are currently teaching.

Certification categories can add up to greater than 100% because some educators have more than one type of certification.

"Certification" is official certification (probationary, temporary certifications are not included).

Certification subjects for elementary school include:

  • Generalist
  • ELA/Bilingual ( if applicable per source system)
  • Special Education ( if applicable per source system)

Certification subjects for middle school and high school include:

  • Core subjects: ELA/Reading, Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies
  • ELA/Bilingual
  • Special Education
  • Generalist (if applicable)
  • Other Special Area

Data Assumptions

  • Certification data is available.
  • Certification data is kept up to date.
  • Teachers who are certified in a subject, but are not currently teaching it, are not included in the percentage.

Data Anomalies

Data Anomalies

  • The date of refresh for teacher metrics differ slightly by campus. This is due to the last date of hire for each campus being different. 
  • If a campus does not have teachers in a subject area, the denominator for this metric may appear different than in other subject areas.
  • Teachers are designated as such in the teacher metrics and teacher list only if they have a section assigned to them.

Implementation Considerations

Teacher Identity

Maintaining a correct and consistent teacher identity is important given that relevant teacher data resides in a number of systems: human resources systems, financial systems, teacher certification systems and class scheduling systems, just to name a few. Most systems use some sort of unique identifier. However, sometimes this identifier is entered incorrectly or sometimes different systems use different identifiers.

The UDM XML supports the interchange of multiple types of identifiers. The TeacherReference is a complex type within the UDM to maintain the referential integrity of the teacher identity (that is, ensuring that the data associated with each teacher is accurately associated with the right teacher). The complex type of the teacher reference assists with implementing the accurate matching algorithm to identify a teacher by utilizing any of the individual attributes (e.g. State Unique Staff ID, Staff ID, Name and Birth Date). For example, if the State Unique Staff ID is unknown, you can find the teachers' identity by their Staff ID, First Name, Last Name and Birth Date.  

Other Considerations

  • Depending on the district/campus, developers may also want to track other key certifications, for example: vocational, gifted/talented, etc.
  • Each state has different types of certifications that qualify for each of the broader categories, map and categorize these appropriately.
  • Certification changes every year and need remapping to account for these changes.
  • Older certification categories may be difficult to map and/or track.
